Background

A system built for a different era.

The Planning Inspectorate's case management system had been running for over 15 years. It was built at a time when infrastructure applications were far simpler — fewer stakeholders, smaller datasets, and nothing like the volume of public consultation responses seen today.

By the time we came on board, case officers were spending hours each week navigating workarounds, manually copying data between screens, and chasing updates across disconnected inboxes. The system wasn't just slow — it was actively getting in the way of good decisions being made on time.

Our brief was to understand the real pain points before touching anything, then rebuild with clarity and scale in mind.

Core pain points

Inefficient and time‑consuming processes.

Working within a complex system and tight deadline, I focused on high-impact decisions, what to keep, and what to redesign. By mapping workflows and pain points and prioritising key opportunities, the Case Admin team emerged as the most critical group to improve.

01

Buried Actions

Actions such as editing and publishing were buried behind multiple clicks, with high interaction costs.

02

Lack of status visibility

Documents had to be manually checked and renamed, with no clear view of their status before redaction.

03

Inability to bulk edit

Every task had to be done one file at a time, adding to the workload and timeframe for completing the work.

Hypothesis

If we enable the ability to edit and publish documents in bulk it will make it quicker to upload and publish files and speed up the planning application process.

A simpler, faster system — built around how people actually work.
