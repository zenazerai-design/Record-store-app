01 · Financial Ombudsman Service

Case summarisation for investigators: two interface bets.

This pitch stays close to people doing case work: high volume, dense evidence, and the need to move between “what happened here” and “what does precedent look like.” The design work was not to slap a chat window on a legacy grid. It was to decide where AI earns space on the screen, how to signal uncertainty without undermining trust, and how to keep every claim inspectable.

Intelligent case search explores natural language across historic cases and the knowledge base, with a search result analysis block placed deliberately above the raw list so synthesis is the headline act, not an afterthought. Live summary splits the investigator view between a running summary, suggested next steps, and suggested outcome, each labeled as AI output with the same explainability pattern (“How this summary was generated”) and calibrated confidence states. Design decisions, pinned to the UI

Tablet exports from the pitch. Width is capped around 1024px so low-resolution PNGs are not stretched full-width on big monitors. For real sharpness on Retina, replace files with ~2048px-wide exports from Figma and keep the same cap: the browser will downscale and use the extra pixels. Hover or tap a marker for crit notes.

Semantic search across cases

Question-shaped search

The field carries a full interrogative (“what has prevented claims from progressing?”), not just keywords. The decision is to match how investigators already talk and to signal semantic retrieval rather than a dumb index.

Synthesis before the list

The “Search result analysis” card sits above the sources. That ordering states priority: read the distilled view first, then open PDFs. It is the clearest retrieval-augmented layout bet in the pitch.

Confidence and provenance

“High confidence,” “Based on 4 historic cases,” the sparkle plus “AI generated summary,” and “How this summary was generated” share one band. Nothing in that band is decorative: it is the minimum viable trust kit next to a bold summary.

Filters stay manual

Source type and sector stay visible rather than folded into model-only behaviour. Investigators keep an explicit contract about scope instead of guessing what the system silently excluded.

Every row cites a source

Decision reference, metadata, and “go to source document in efile” ground the analysis in artifacts people can open. That is the anti-hallucination pattern for this domain.

Historic search: hierarchy, trust band, grounding.

Live summary workspace

Two modes of help

“Live summary” versus “Suggestions” keeps reading the file separate from accepting proposed moves. Collapsing those modes would force investigators to trust one undifferentiated block.

Calibrated uncertainty

“Medium confidence” pairs with a rationale such as thin evidence or possible mischaracterisation. The UI treats doubt as a prompt to act, not as fine print to ignore.

Depth and inspectability

Simplify, Go Deeper, View Sources, and Select text expose depth as a control surface. View Sources is the same grounding instinct as the efile links on the search screen.

Dialogue, not a monologue

The chat field and suggested chips phrase follow-ups as investigation questions. The flow assumes another turn, which mirrors real case work.

Usefulness as interface grammar

“Was this summary useful?” lives in the canvas, not only in a post-intercept survey. For the pitch it signals intent to close the loop between investigators and model quality.

Designing for AI

Final thoughts on designing for AI.

  • Automation with transparency. If the product summarizes, ranks, or shortcuts work, the UI should show what happened in plain language — provenance, scope, confidence where it matters — not hide it behind a sparkle and a paragraph nobody can check.
  • Let people choose AI — and choose out. The non-AI path stays visible: full lists, manual filters, investigator-first layouts. Switching to “recommended” or assistive modes should feel like a deliberate toggle, not the only door in.
  • Don’t be pushy. Nudge quietly: confirm intent before personalization, avoid piling “smart” defaults on every step, and treat assistance as something the user can ignore without penalty. Pushy AI reads as a funnel, not a partner.
